United States Space Force

America's newest military branch, created through normal legislative process, not a secret operation

America's Newest Military Branch

The United States Space Force (USSF) was established on December 20, 2019, when President Trump signed the National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) for Fiscal Year 2020 into law. It was the first new military branch created since the U.S. Air Force was established in 1947.

The creation of the Space Force had bipartisan support in Congress and was passed through the normal legislative process as part of the NDAA, not through executive order or secret military action.

Official Sources: Everything in this chapter comes from spaceforce.mil, the official Space Force website. In 2026, the Space Force is focused on building resilient space architecture, combat readiness briefings at the AFA Warfare Symposium, and training command strategic vision. None of this involves domestic governance, martial law, or secret government operations. The Space Force protects American interests in space. That is their job. It is publicly documented.

The Long Road to a Space Branch

The idea of a dedicated space military branch was not new when Trump signed it into law. The concept has roots going back decades:

  • 1945: The U.S. Army Air Forces published the “Toward New Horizons” report, identifying space as a future domain of military operations.
  • Cold War era: The space race between the U.S. and Soviet Union drove massive investment in military space capabilities.
  • 1982: Air Force Space Command was established to manage Air Force space operations.
  • 1985: Naval Space Command was established to coordinate Navy space activities.
  • 1988: U.S. Space Command was established as a unified combatant command to coordinate all military space operations.
  • 2010s: Various proposals emerged in Congress for a Space Guard, Space Corps, and Space Force as space became increasingly contested.
  • February 2019: President Trump signed Space Policy Directive 4, directing the Pentagon to establish a Space Force.
  • December 20, 2019: Space Force officially established through the NDAA with bipartisan Congressional support.

Mission Statement

On September 6, 2023, the Space Force announced its official mission statement:

Mission: “Secure our Nation's interests in, from, and to space.”

This mission is organized around three core functions:

  • In space: Achieving and maintaining space superiority to protect U.S. and allied assets in orbit.
  • From space: Delivering capabilities from space to support operations on Earth, including satellite communications, GPS navigation, missile warning, and environmental monitoring.
  • To space: Maintaining the infrastructure and capability to launch payloads into orbit, including rockets, satellites, and support systems.

Organizational Structure

The Space Force uses a four-tiered organizational structure designed for the unique demands of space operations:

  • USSF Headquarters: Overall strategic direction and policy.
  • Field Commands: Major operational and support organizations.
  • Space Deltas: Comparable to wings in the Air Force, organized around specific mission areas.
  • Squadrons: The fundamental operational units of the Space Force.

Three Field Commands

Space Operations Command (SpOC)

The operational arm of the Space Force, responsible for generating, presenting, and sustaining space forces. SpOC commands the Space Deltas that conduct day-to-day space operations including satellite control, space domain awareness, missile warning, and electronic warfare.

Space Systems Command (SSC)

The acquisition and development arm of the Space Force with a $15.6 billion annual budget. SSC is responsible for developing, acquiring, and fielding lethal and resilient space capabilities. It manages the entire lifecycle of space systems from research through deployment.

Space Training and Readiness Command (STARCOM)

Activated on August 23, 2021, STARCOM is responsible for training, education, and doctrine development for the Space Force. It ensures Guardians are prepared for the unique demands of space operations through specialized training programs and exercises.

How Grifters Misrepresent Space Force

Derek Johnson and other influencers frequently cite the Space Force as "proof" that Trump was building a secret military apparatus. Here's what they claim vs. reality:

Grifter ClaimReality
"Trump created Space Force for the secret operation"Space Force was created through bipartisan NDAA legislation, not executive order. Congress voted on it.
"Space Force monitors the election through satellites"Space Force operates GPS, missile warning, and communications satellites. None monitor domestic elections.
"Space Force proves Trump is still Commander in Chief"Space Force operates under the current Commander in Chief, whoever that is. The branch doesn't answer to former presidents.
"The Space Force flag ceremony was a secret signal"Every new military branch gets a flag ceremony. The Air Force got one in 1947. It's protocol, not code.
The Space Force was created through normal legislative process with bipartisan support. It reorganized existing military space capabilities that were fragmented across multiple branches; it did not create a secret military operation. It is the smallest branch of the military with approximately 14,000 personnel, focused on satellite operations and space domain awareness.
