250 Years of Survival

Before we talk about today's threats, look at what the Constitution has already survived. Not theories about threats. Real ones.

1798
The Alien and Sedition Acts
Seven years after the Bill of Rights, President John Adams made it illegal to criticize the government. The Constitution survived. The laws expired. Adams lost re-election.
1861-1865
The Civil War
Eleven states tried to leave the Union. Over 600,000 Americans died. The Constitution survived and came out stronger with the 13th, 14th, and 15th Amendments.
1942
Japanese American Internment
120,000 Japanese Americans forced into camps by executive order. The Constitution survived. The government formally apologized decades later.
1950s
McCarthyism
Thousands accused of being communists with no evidence. Careers destroyed. Lives ruined. The Constitution survived. McCarthy was censured. Today's conspiracy promoters are tomorrow's McCarthys.
1972-1974
Watergate
President Nixon authorized illegal surveillance. The constitutional system worked exactly as designed: Congress investigated, the Supreme Court ordered the tapes released, Nixon resigned. No secret military plan was needed.
2001
The Patriot Act
Fear drove sweeping surveillance powers. Snowden exposed mass domestic surveillance. Courts struck down portions of it. The system corrected itself. Again.
Every one of these was a real constitutional crisis. In every case, the system corrected itself. Not quickly. Not perfectly. But it corrected. The Constitution does not need a secret military plan to protect it. It has courts, Congress, the press, and the people. You are one of those people. That is why you are here.
