CHAPTER 10B

The On-Ramp

How mainstream voices radicalize audiences, and why Candace Owens is the most dangerous example

She Liked a Post About Drinking Christian Blood

Read that headline again. A woman with millions of followers, a woman who was on Fox News, a woman who was the face of Turning Point USA, a woman who sat across from the President of the United States, liked a social media post asking a rabbi if he was "drunk on Christian blood again."

That is the blood libel. It is one of the oldest antisemitic lies in human history. It dates back to the 12th century. It was used to justify the murder of Jewish communities across Europe for 800 years. Pogroms. Expulsions. Massacres. All justified by the lie that Jews drink Christian blood. And Candace Owens liked it on X for her millions of followers to see.

How did she get there? How does someone go from mainstream conservative commentator to promoting medieval antisemitic conspiracy theories? That is what this chapter is about. Because Candace Owens did not start here. She was walked here, step by step, in public, with receipts. And she took millions of people with her.

The grifters in Chapter 11 operate in a world most Americans never see. Telegram channels. Rumble livestreams. Paid Substacks. Those audiences are already captured. Candace Owens is more dangerous than all of them combined because she had millions of mainstream followers who were NOT in the conspiracy ecosystem. They were watching Fox News. They were reading conservative commentary. They were engaged in normal political discourse. And she walked them, one post at a time, toward the same destination as every other conspiracy pipeline on this site.

The Radicalization Timeline

2017-2020
Mainstream Conservative Commentator
Owens builds a massive following as a conservative voice. She works with Turning Point USA, appears on Fox News regularly, and becomes one of the most recognizable Black conservative figures in America. Her content focuses on mainstream conservative issues: free markets, personal responsibility, criticism of the Democratic Party. At this point, she is operating within the bounds of legitimate political commentary.
2021-2022
Daily Wire Era
Owens joins The Daily Wire, Ben Shapiro's media company, and gets her own show. She has a massive platform with millions of followers across social media. She begins pushing the boundaries of mainstream commentary, making increasingly provocative statements. The audience grows. The engagement grows. The revenue grows.
Oct 2023
The October 7th Pivot
After Hamas attacks Israel on October 7, 2023, Owens begins criticizing Israel's response in Gaza. This is legitimate commentary that many people across the political spectrum share. But Owens does not stop at policy criticism. She begins framing the conflict using conspiracy language: shadowy forces controlling the narrative, media manipulation, suppressed truths. The framework is familiar to anyone who has read Chapters 4, 5, and 9 of this site.
Nov 2023
"You Cannot Serve Both God and Money"
Ben Shapiro, who is Jewish, publicly calls her comments "disgraceful." Owens fires back with a biblical quote: "You cannot serve both God and money." Think about that. She is directing a Bible verse about greed at her Jewish employer. She follows it with a two-word post: "Christ is King." In isolation, a statement of faith that billions of Christians affirm. In context, aimed directly at Jewish colleagues who just criticized her, it is a weapon. Daily Wire co-founder Jeremy Boreing spelled it out: the phrase becomes antisemitic "when it is used for the purpose of expressing antisemitism." Owens knew exactly what she was doing. Her audience knew exactly what she meant. And the engagement numbers went through the roof.
Early 2024
The Blood Libel
Owens likes a post on X asking Rabbi Shmuley Boteach if he was "drunk on Christian blood again." This is not a political disagreement. This is the blood libel, one of the oldest and most dangerous antisemitic conspiracy theories in history. It dates back to the 12th century and has been used to justify pogroms, expulsions, and massacres of Jewish communities across Europe for 800 years. Owens liked it on social media for her millions of followers to see.
March 22, 2024
Fired from The Daily Wire
The Daily Wire severs ties with Owens. CEO Jeremy Boreing announces "Daily Wire and Candace Owens have ended their relationship." Owens responds: "The rumors are true. I am finally free." She frames her firing as liberation, not consequences. This is the grifter playbook: accountability becomes persecution, and persecution becomes proof that you were over the target.
Sept 2024
The Rabbi Shmuley Debate
Owens debates Rabbi Shmuley Boteach on Piers Morgan's show. During the debate, she calls the Chabad-Lubavitch movement's revered leader, Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson, "a hate-monger who preached Jewish supremacism." She is no longer making political arguments. She is attacking the religious leaders of a faith community to an audience of millions. The ADL formally designated her as promoting explicitly antisemitic conspiracy theories.
2025-2026
Full Conspiracy Ecosystem Integration
Free from Daily Wire's editorial guardrails, Owens goes deeper. She promotes "secret Jewish gangs" in Hollywood narratives. She platforms conspiracy figures. She uses the same framework as every other conspiracy promoter covered on this site: shadowy elites, suppressed truth, mainstream media as the enemy, and herself as the brave truth-teller being persecuted for asking questions. The on-ramp is now a highway.

Why This Is More Dangerous Than the Grifters

Here is why Candace Owens is more dangerous than every grifter in Chapter 11 combined.

Dave Fishman (X22 Report) has 810,000 Rumble followers. Derek Johnson has 110,000. Jon Herold has 115,000. Charlie Ward claims 13 million but his actual engaged audience is a fraction of that. These are large numbers, but they are niche audiences who actively sought out conspiracy content. They were already in the ecosystem.

Candace Owens had millions of mainstream followers who were NOT in the conspiracy ecosystem. They were watching Fox News. They were reading mainstream conservative commentary. They were engaged in normal political discourse. And she walked them, step by step, from legitimate conservatism toward antisemitic conspiracy theories.

The grifters in Chapter 11 preach to the converted. Candace Owens converts the unconverted. That is the difference between a street preacher and a recruiter. The street preacher talks to people who already believe. The recruiter finds people who do not believe yet and brings them in. Owens is a recruiter, and she is one of the most effective ones the conspiracy ecosystem has ever had.

The On-Ramp Pipeline

This is how the radicalization pipeline works, and Owens is the entry point for millions:

Step 1: Legitimate Grievance "The media is biased. Government is too big. My concerns are dismissed."
Step 2: Mainstream Commentator Validates Owens says what you are thinking. She has credentials. She is on TV. She must be right.
Step 3: The Framework Shifts "It's not just bias. There are forces controlling the narrative. They don't want you to know."
Step 4: The Enemy Gets Named "Globalist elites. Shadowy cabals. People who 'serve money.' Secret gangs in Hollywood."
Step 5: The Destination The "globalist elites" are identified as Jewish. The "cabals" are Rothschilds and Soros. You are now consuming the same content as the people in Chapters 9 and 10.

Most people who end up at Step 5 do not realize how they got there. They did not set out to consume antisemitic content. They started by agreeing with a conservative commentator about media bias or government overreach. Those are legitimate concerns. But the pipeline did not keep them at the legitimate concern. It moved them, one step at a time, toward a destination they never chose.

The Fracture: When She Turned on Her Own

The radicalization pipeline does not just pull people toward conspiracy content. It turns them against the very people and organizations that built them. Candace Owens was made by Turning Point USA. Charlie Kirk gave her a platform. TPUSA events gave her audiences. That organization was the foundation of her career in conservative media.

Now she is actively attacking TPUSA and going after Erika Kirk. She has turned against the organization that launched her and the people who supported her. This is what radicalization does. It does not just change what you believe. It changes who you are willing to destroy. The people she once stood beside are now targets because they did not follow her down the rabbit hole.

This is not a political disagreement. Political disagreements happen within a shared framework. This is a fracture where one side has left the framework entirely. When someone who built their career on conservative activism starts attacking other conservative activists because they refuse to embrace conspiracy theories and antisemitism, the radicalization is complete. The pipeline has done its work.

This is not theoretical. This is happening inside families right now. I have family members who believe Candace Owens and hate Erika Kirk, even though they support Trump. They agree on the President. They agree on policy. They agree on the direction of the country. And they cannot sit in the same room because one side followed Candace down the rabbit hole and the other did not. Parents against children. Siblings against siblings. Lifelong friends who can no longer have a conversation without it turning into a fight about whether "the Jews control everything." Not because they disagree on politics. Because one of them now believes things that the other cannot accept. The conspiracy industry does not just take your money. It takes your family. It takes your friendships. It takes the people you love and turns them into strangers who share your last name.

She Is Not the Only One

Owens is the most documented case, but she is not alone. The on-ramp pattern appears whenever a mainstream figure begins using conspiracy frameworks:

Tucker Carlson

Amplified the "Great Replacement" theory on the most-watched cable news show in America. His audience of millions heard conspiracy-adjacent framing from a man in a suit on a major network. They had no idea they were consuming repackaged conspiracy content because it came from a trusted source. When the most-watched cable news host echoes your framework, it stops looking fringe and starts looking like news.

Megyn Kelly

Platformed conspiracy figures and gave them credibility through association. When a former Fox News and NBC anchor interviews someone, the audience assumes that person has been vetted. The credibility of the host transfers to the guest, regardless of whether the guest deserves it.

Roseanne Barr

Went from beloved comedian to conspiracy amplifier. Lost her ABC sitcom over racist tweets, then went deeper into conspiracy content rather than stepping back. Appeared at Turning Point USA events and cultivated ties with Juan O'Savin (Wayne Willott, the insurance investigator whose followers believe he is JFK Jr.). Celebrity name recognition gives fringe content mainstream visibility.

Jim Caviezel

The actor who played Jesus Christ in "The Passion of the Christ" used that role's cultural weight to promote conspiracy content. When the man who played Jesus validates a conspiracy figure, it creates a religious endorsement that bypasses critical thinking entirely. Audiences do not see a celebrity promoting conspiracy content. They see the face of Christ telling them the truth.

How to Recognize the On-Ramp

Here are the warning signs that a mainstream voice is becoming an on-ramp to radicalization:

  • The shift from policy to conspiracy. Criticizing a government policy is legitimate. Claiming that shadowy forces are secretly controlling the policy is a conspiracy framework. Watch for when the language shifts from "this policy is wrong" to "they don't want you to know."
  • The persecution narrative. When a commentator gets fired, loses a platform, or faces consequences, watch how they frame it. If they say "I made a mistake," that is accountability. If they say "they are trying to silence me because I am over the target," that is the grifter playbook.
  • The unnamed enemy. Legitimate criticism names specific people, policies, and institutions. Conspiracy frameworks use vague, ominous language: "the elites," "the globalists," "they." When "they" cannot be named, you are consuming a conspiracy narrative.
  • The monetization pivot. After losing mainstream platforms, watch whether the person pivots to direct monetization: paid subscriptions, merchandise, speaking tours, pay-per-view content. The persecution narrative becomes the brand. The brand generates revenue. The revenue creates an incentive to go deeper.
  • The cross-promotion. Watch who they start associating with. If a mainstream figure begins appearing alongside people covered in Chapter 11, they have entered the ecosystem. The association is the signal.
The bottom line: You can criticize Israel's policies without being antisemitic. You can criticize media bias without promoting conspiracy theories. You can question your government without claiming it has been secretly overthrown. The on-ramp works by blurring these distinctions until legitimate concern and conspiracy theory become indistinguishable. Once that line is erased, the pipeline takes over. Recognizing the on-ramp is the first step to staying off it.
