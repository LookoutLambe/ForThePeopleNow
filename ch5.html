How They Get You

Nobody sets out to believe a conspiracy theory. Nobody wakes up one morning and decides the Constitution has been secretly suspended. It happens gradually, and it starts with something real.

Sex trafficking is real. So they attach it to a theory about secret military tribunals that are going to arrest everyone involved any day now. Government corruption is real. So they attach it to a theory about a shadow government secretly running the country. Election integrity is a legitimate concern. So they attach it to a theory about three cannons at an inauguration proving the president is fake. Every real issue becomes bait for a fabricated theory.

And here is the damage that nobody talks about. When someone who cares about trafficking starts promoting theories about 3-cannon salutes and military government, the trafficking issue gets discredited by association. When someone who cares about accountability starts promoting theories decoded by an anonymous poster on 8chan, the accountability movement looks unhinged. The conspiracy industry does not help the causes it claims to champion. It exploits them for revenue and discredits them in the process. The real issues deserve serious attention from serious people, not monetized content from people who have never done the work.
