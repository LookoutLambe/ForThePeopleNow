CHAPTER 13

Finding the Truth

The resolution. How to find real sources, verify claims, and take your country back for real.

This Chapter Is Different

The previous twelve chapters showed you what is wrong. This chapter shows you what is right. It is not enough to know who the grifters are. You need to know who the real analysts are. It is not enough to know that a claim is false. You need to know how to verify claims yourself so you never have to depend on someone else to tell you what is true.

This is the resolution. The conspiracy industry profits from keeping you passive, afraid, and dependent on their content. This chapter is about making you independent. When you can read a government document yourself, you do not need a Substack blogger to interpret it for you. When you can verify a claim in five minutes, you do not need to pay for a livestream to hear someone's opinion about it. Knowledge is free. The only people who charge for it are the ones who do not want you to find it on your own.

Go to the Source: Read It Yourself

Every claim made by every conspiracy theorist on this site can be verified or debunked by reading the actual documents they cite. The documents are publicly available. You do not need a Substack subscription to read them. You do not need a pay-per-view stream to understand them. You just need to read them.

The U.S. Constitution

Read it at senate.gov. Every claim about constitutional authority, military power, and government structure can be verified here. If someone tells you the Constitution says something, read it yourself. It is not long.

Executive Orders

Read them at whitehouse.gov. Every executive order is published in full text. When someone claims an EO proves devolution or military government, read the actual EO. It will say what it says, not what a Substack blogger claims it says.

FEMA Continuity Documents

Read them at fema.gov. Federal Continuity Directives, the Continuity Guidance Circular, and the National Continuity Policy are all publicly available. This is what real continuity planning looks like, not what conspiracy theorists claim it looks like.

Congressional Research Service Reports

Read them at crsreports.congress.gov. CRS reports are nonpartisan analyses written for Congress. They cover continuity of government, executive orders, military authority, and every other topic the conspiracy industry misrepresents. These reports are written by professional researchers, not anonymous accounts.

The Law of War Manual

Read it at dod.defense.gov. Derek Johnson cites this document constantly. Read the foreword. It states explicitly that it covers the conduct of hostilities and the protection of war victims. Not domestic governance. Not military occupation of America. Read it yourself and compare what it says to what DJ claims it says.

Congress.gov

Read actual legislation at congress.gov. Every law, every bill, every resolution is published here. When someone claims a law says something, you can read the actual text in minutes.

Five Questions That Expose Every Grifter

You do not need a degree in emergency management or a military career to identify conspiracy content. You just need five questions.

1. What are their credentials?

Did this person serve in the military? In what capacity? For how long? What was their MOS? Did they have a security clearance? Did they study the topic professionally? A 14S National Guard soldier with 21 months of service is not a credible source on classified continuity protocols. A computer technician is not a military analyst. A woman with a diploma mill PhD is not an academic authority. Credentials matter.

2. Are they charging money?

If someone genuinely believed a secret military operation was underway to save the country, sharing that information freely would be a patriotic duty. Instead, they put it behind paywalls, sell books, charge for livestreams, and monetize every platform they operate on. If the information were real, it would be free. The paywall tells you everything you need to know about what they actually believe.

3. What is their track record?

How many of their predictions have come true? Mass arrests? Military tribunals? Emergency Broadcast System activation? Currency resets? If someone has made hundreds of predictions and zero have been fulfilled, that is not a source. That is a slot machine that never pays out but keeps taking your quarters.

4. Can you read the source document?

When someone claims an executive order proves something, can you read the executive order yourself? If yes, read it and compare what it says to what they claim. If they discourage you from reading the source document and insist you need their interpretation, that is a red flag. Real analysts point you to the source. Grifters point you to their Substack.

5. What happens when they are wrong?

When a prediction fails, do they acknowledge it and adjust? Or do they move the goalposts, blame the deep state, and make a new prediction? Real analysts admit when they are wrong because their credibility depends on accuracy. Grifters never admit they are wrong because their revenue depends on the narrative continuing regardless of results.

Stop Watching. Start Doing.

The conspiracy industry wants you watching livestreams. Democracy needs you attending town halls. The conspiracy industry wants you decoding anonymous posts. Democracy needs you reading legislation. The conspiracy industry wants you waiting for someone else to save the country. Democracy needs you to do it yourself.

Here is what actually changes things:

  • Vote in every election. Not just presidential elections. Local elections, primaries, school board races, city council. The people who control your daily life are elected at the local level, and most Americans do not vote in those races.
  • Attend local government meetings. City council, county commission, school board. These meetings are open to the public. Show up. Ask questions. Hold officials accountable in person, not on social media.
  • Read the actual documents. Executive orders, legislation, court rulings. They are all publicly available. If someone tells you a document says something, read it yourself. It takes less time than watching a livestream.
  • Support local journalism. Local reporters cover the government officials who affect your life most directly. When local journalism dies, accountability dies with it. Subscribe to your local paper.
  • Talk to your neighbors. The conspiracy industry isolates people in online echo chambers. Real community happens in person. Talk to the people around you. You have more in common than the internet wants you to believe.
Nobody is coming to save you. That is not a reason to give up. It is a reason to get involved. The Constitution does not work by itself. It works because citizens make it work. Every right you have was fought for by Americans who showed up, spoke up, and did the work. The grifters want you to sit at home and watch their shows. The Founders wanted you to govern yourselves. Choose the Founders.
