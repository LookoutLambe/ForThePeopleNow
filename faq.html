Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions and clear, sourced answers about the topics covered on this site.

Is it possible for the President and the Commander In Chief to be two separate individuals?

No, the President and the Commander in Chief are the same person. The Constitution states that the President is the Commander in Chief of the United States Armed Forces, including the Army, Navy, and Militia. The President has supreme operational command and control over the military. The President is also the head of state and head of government. The President's power as Commander in Chief comes from the Commander in Chief Clause (Article II).

This is one of the most frequently misunderstood concepts in the conspiracy space. Content creators like Derek Johnson claim that Trump remained Commander in Chief after leaving office while Biden served as a separate "president." This is constitutionally impossible. The moment a new president is sworn in under the 20th Amendment, that person becomes Commander in Chief. There is no mechanism in the Constitution, in federal law, or in military regulations to separate the two roles. See Chapter 1: Constitutional Warfare and Chapter 5: The Law of War Manual.

Is it possible for a President to write an Executive Order to make themselves the Commander In Chief?

No. The President is automatically the Commander in Chief by virtue of holding the office of the presidency, as established by Article II of the Constitution. An executive order cannot grant a power that is already inherently vested in the office by the Constitution. Executive orders are directives that manage operations of the federal government. They cannot create new constitutional powers.

This question comes up because conspiracy theorists point to various executive orders as supposed proof that Trump activated secret military powers. Executive orders derive their authority from either the Constitution or from statutes passed by Congress. They cannot expand presidential power beyond what the Constitution already grants. Any executive order that attempted to do so would be unconstitutional and unenforceable. See Chapter 1.

Why hasn't the military intervened?

The Posse Comitatus Act (18 U.S.C. § 1385), signed on June 18, 1878, by President Rutherford B. Hayes, limits the powers of the federal government in the use of federal military personnel to enforce domestic policies within the United States. This is a fundamental legal restriction that prevents the military from intervening in civilian governance and elections. When operating in Title 32 status, National Guard forces are exempt from the Posse Comitatus Act because they are under state command and control, but this does not authorize them to intervene in elections or governance.

The entire premise of devolution theory and Derek Johnson's narrative relies on the military secretly running the government. Every service member takes an oath to support and defend the Constitution, not a political party or an individual. The military chain of command runs from the sitting President through the Secretary of Defense. Active duty military members who disobeyed the lawful orders of the current Commander in Chief would be subject to court martial under the Uniform Code of Military Justice. See Chapter 2: The Military Oath.

Is devolution real?

Yes, devolution is a real concept, but it is nothing like what Jon Herold describes. In emergency management, devolution is the planned transfer of essential government functions to alternate personnel at a different location when the primary staff and facilities are unavailable. FEMA defines it in Federal Continuity Directive 1 (FCD-1) as a component of Continuity of Operations (COOP) planning. It is a civilian-led, constitutional, and entirely bureaucratic process designed to keep agencies functioning during emergencies like natural disasters or attacks on the national capital.

Jon Herold's version of "devolution" claims that Trump secretly transferred military authority over the government before leaving office, that the military is covertly running the country, and that Biden's presidency was somehow illegitimate. None of this has any basis in actual devolution planning. Real devolution plans are documented in agency COOP plans, follow the constitutional order of succession, and are designed to preserve the existing government structure, not overthrow it. Herold never once properly defined devolution using FEMA's own framework in his 25-part series, because the real definition would destroy his theory.

See Chapter 4: Devolution Theory for the full breakdown of how Herold's theory diverges from reality, and Chapter 6: Continuity of Government for how real continuity planning actually works.

What are PEADs and did Trump use them to stay in power?

Presidential Emergency Action Documents (PEADs) are pre-drafted executive orders, proclamations, and messages prepared for emergency scenarios. They've existed since the Eisenhower administration. They are classified, and no PEAD has ever been declassified or leaked. Jon Herold built his theory on speculation about what PEADs contain. He admits no one knows what's in them, then proceeds to guess. The Brennan Center for Justice has published research on PEADs confirming they are administrative preparation documents, not secret military activation orders.

Here is the critical logical problem with the PEADs argument: if they are classified and no one outside of a small circle of senior officials has ever seen them, then Jon Herold, a civilian with no security clearance, cannot possibly know what they contain. He cannot build a theory on documents he has never read. His entire argument amounts to saying "these secret documents could contain anything, so let me tell you what I think they say." That is not research. That is speculation repackaged as analysis. See Chapter 4: The PEADs Trick.

Does EO 13848 prove the election was stolen?

No. EO 13848 itself states: "Although there has been no evidence of a foreign power altering the outcome or vote tabulation in any United States election." Trump's own executive order says there was no evidence of altered outcomes. The EO established a process for sanctions against foreign interference: sanctions, export restrictions, and asset freezing. Not military intervention. Read the full EO (PDF).

Conspiracy theorists treat EO 13848 as a trigger mechanism for military action, but the executive order itself lays out a diplomatic and economic response framework. It directs the Director of National Intelligence to produce an assessment, after which the Treasury and State Departments may impose sanctions. There is no mention of military intervention, martial law, or seizure of voting machines anywhere in the document. Anyone who claims otherwise has either not read it or is counting on the fact that their audience has not read it. See Chapter 4.

What is the Law of War Manual actually for?

The Department of Defense Law of War Manual is a reference guide for military lawyers (Judge Advocate General officers) and commanders on the legal obligations of the United States during armed conflict with foreign powers. It covers topics such as the treatment of prisoners of war, the conduct of hostilities, the rules governing naval warfare, and the legal status of occupied territories in the context of international armed conflict. It is a legal reference document, not an operational playbook.

Derek Johnson cherry-picks sections of the manual, particularly those dealing with military occupation, and claims they prove the U.S. is currently under military occupation. The manual itself explicitly states that it "focuses on jus in bello, law relating to the conduct of hostilities" in international armed conflict. It was written to guide U.S. forces operating overseas against foreign adversaries, not to describe domestic governance. The manual also contains a disclaimer that it does not create any enforceable legal rights or benefits. Johnson ignores all of this context. See Chapter 5: The Law of War Manual.

Does the Law of War Manual prove we're under military occupation?

No. The Law of War Manual itself contains these disclaimers: "This manual is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit, substantive or procedural, enforceable at law or in equity." It also states it "focuses on jus in bello, law relating to the conduct of hostilities" (international armed conflict, not domestic governance) and is "not a substitute for the careful practice of law." The document Derek Johnson built his brand on literally says it can't be used the way he uses it. See Chapter 5.

Is the United States a corporation?

No. The District of Columbia Organic Act of 1871 reorganized the local government of Washington D.C. The word "corporation" refers to a municipal corporation, the legal term for a city government. Your hometown is a municipal corporation. The Act didn't touch the Constitution, didn't change the national government, and was actually part of post-Civil War Reconstruction. This theory originated from the Posse Comitatus movement, a white supremacist group. The FBI classifies sovereign citizens as a domestic terrorist threat. See Chapter 8: The 1871 Act.

Sovereign citizen ideology claims that the "real" United States government was replaced by a corporation in 1871, and that citizens can free themselves from this corporate government by filing certain legal documents or using specific language in court. This has never worked. Courts at every level have rejected sovereign citizen arguments. People who have tried to use these arguments have been fined, held in contempt, and imprisoned. The theory is not just wrong; following its legal advice can result in serious consequences for anyone who tries it. See Chapter 8.

Is the military in control?

No. The United States has maintained unbroken civilian control of the military since its founding. This is one of the most fundamental principles of the American system of government. The President, a civilian elected official, serves as Commander in Chief. The Secretary of Defense is a civilian. By law, a former military officer must wait seven years after retirement before being eligible to serve as Secretary of Defense, specifically to preserve civilian control. Congress holds the power to declare war and controls the military budget.

The idea that the military is secretly running the country while maintaining the appearance of normal civilian government requires believing that every general, every admiral, every service secretary, and the entire Joint Chiefs of Staff are engaged in a coordinated deception of the American people. It also requires believing that thousands of senior military leaders are willing to violate their oath to support and defend the Constitution. Every service member who has served knows that the military follows orders from civilian leadership. That is not a flaw in the system; it is the system. What is NESARA/GESARA?

NESARA stands for the National Economic Security and Reformation Act. It was a real legislative proposal drafted by Harvey Barnard in the 1990s that was never introduced in Congress, never voted on, and never signed into law. It proposed monetary and tax reforms. After Barnard's proposal went nowhere, a woman named Shaini Candace Goodwin (who went by "Dove of Oneness") transformed it into a conspiracy theory claiming that NESARA had been secretly signed into law by Bill Clinton, that it would eliminate all debts, abolish the IRS, and return the country to a gold standard, but that the September 11 attacks were orchestrated to prevent its announcement.

GESARA is the supposed global version. Neither exists as law. No government has acknowledged either. The theory has been recycled for over two decades, with the "announcement date" perpetually moved forward. It is a financial fantasy that keeps people waiting for a debt jubilee that will never come. Content creators promote it because it gives their audience hope and keeps them engaged. If someone is telling you NESARA/GESARA is about to be announced, they are either misinformed or they are selling you something. See Chapter 11: The Conspiracy Industry.

Is the earth flat?

No. The earth is an oblate spheroid, slightly wider at the equator than at the poles. This has been known since ancient Greek astronomers calculated the earth's circumference over 2,000 years ago. Every space agency on the planet, every airline, every naval fleet, every GPS satellite, and every telecommunications company operates on the basis of a spherical earth. Flat earth theory requires believing that every government, every scientist, every pilot, every sailor, and every engineer in the world is part of a coordinated deception spanning centuries.

Flat earth is included on this site because it represents the far end of the conspiracy pipeline. People who start by questioning one mainstream narrative can, over time, be led to question increasingly basic aspects of reality. The same pattern of thinking that leads someone to believe the election was stolen or that the military is secretly in control can eventually lead them to reject observable, measurable physical reality. The conspiracy industry profits from this progression because the deeper someone goes, the more content they consume and the more products they buy. See Chapter 12: Flat Earth.

What happened on January 6th?

The certification process was delayed for hours before resuming and completing that evening. Over 1,200 people have been charged with federal crimes related to the breach.

How do I know if I'm reading misinformation?

There are several reliable indicators. First, check the sources. Does the author cite official government documents, court records, or peer-reviewed research, or do they cite other conspiracy theorists and anonymous sources? Second, check the credentials. Does the person have actual expertise in the subject they are discussing, or are they self-taught via YouTube and social media? Third, check the predictions. Has this person's previous analysis been accurate, or do they constantly move the goalposts when their predictions fail? Fourth, check the business model. Is the content free and transparent, or is it behind a paywall with upsells?

Misinformation often uses real documents but misrepresents what they say. Derek Johnson quotes the Law of War Manual but ignores its disclaimers. Jon Herold references PEADs but admits no one knows what they contain. The most effective misinformation takes a grain of truth and buries it in speculation. If someone tells you to "do your own research" but then directs you to their own content rather than to primary sources, they are not encouraging independent thinking. They are building a closed information loop where they control the narrative. See Chapter 13: Credible Sources.

Why do people believe conspiracy theories?

Conspiracy theories fulfill basic psychological needs. They provide simple explanations for complex events, giving people a sense of understanding in a confusing world. They create a sense of community and belonging among believers, who see themselves as enlightened insiders. They offer hope that someone powerful is secretly working to fix everything, relieving the individual of the burden of action. Research in social psychology has identified several factors that make people more susceptible, including a need for certainty, a distrust of institutions, a desire to feel special or unique, and exposure to repeated messaging.

The conspiracy pipeline is also designed to be progressive. A person might start by questioning one thing, like election security, and gradually be exposed to increasingly extreme ideas. Each step feels logical because it builds on the previous one. Social media algorithms accelerate this process by showing users more of what they engage with, creating feedback loops that push people deeper into conspiracy content. Understanding this is important because it means that the people who believe these theories are not stupid. They are often intelligent, well-meaning people who were systematically misled by a content ecosystem designed to exploit their concerns. See Chapter 3: Foreign Information Warfare and Chapter 11.
