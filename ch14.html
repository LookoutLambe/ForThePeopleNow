Words Matter: Know the Difference

Misinformation: Wrong information shared by people who don't know it's wrong. Your uncle sharing a false meme because he thought it was real.

Disinformation: Wrong information spread deliberately to manipulate you. A Russian troll farm creating fake American accounts to spread division.

Malinformation: Real information taken out of context to mislead. Taking a real executive order and claiming it means something it doesn't. This is what Patel Patriot and Derek Johnson do.

Who Is Doing This and Why

Russia: The Architect

Russia wrote the playbook on information warfare against democracies. This is not new. Soviet "active measures" go back decades. What is new is the scale and speed that social media provides. The Internet Research Agency, a Kremlin-linked troll farm operating out of St. Petersburg, ran a massive campaign during the 2016 election. They created fake American accounts on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram. They posed as both liberals and conservatives. Their goal was not to support one side. It was to get Americans fighting with each other.

They organized real protests and counter-protests in the same city on the same day. They created Facebook groups for both Black Lives Matter supporters and anti-immigration activists. They amplified the most extreme voices on every side of every issue. And they are still at it. The Mueller investigation documented this in hundreds of pages of evidence. Russia's goal is simple: undermine Americans' faith in democracy itself. Every conspiracy theory that tells you your government is illegitimate accomplishes this goal for free.

China: The Long Game

China plays a different game, but the objective is the same. They run global propaganda operations to promote their authoritarian model as superior to Western democracy. Within China, the internet is completely censored behind the Great Firewall. Now they are expanding that influence abroad, pressuring foreign media companies to self-censor in exchange for access to the Chinese market, running state-sponsored bot farms on Western social media platforms, and using economic leverage to silence criticism. Their information warfare capabilities are growing fast, and they are learning from Russia's successes. Every time an American posts that democracy does not work, that the system is rigged beyond repair, or that constitutional governance has failed, it validates Beijing's central message: that authoritarianism is the superior system.
