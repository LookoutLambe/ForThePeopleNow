Glossary

Quick reference for terms used on this site

This glossary covers the acronyms, legal terms, and concepts referenced throughout the site. If a grifter throws an acronym at you, look it up here first.

A

AGR: Active Guard Reserve
Full-time National Guard or Reserve members who serve in an active-duty capacity to support their unit's mission. They wear the uniform every day but are not "active duty" in the traditional sense.
AIT: Advanced Individual Training
The job-specific training a soldier receives after Basic Combat Training. This is where you learn your MOS (job). Derek Johnson completed AIT for 14S before being injured.

C

COG: Continuity of Government
Plans designed to preserve the constitutional form of government and leadership succession during a catastrophic event. Led by civilian authority, not the military. See Chapter 6.
COOP: Continuity of Operations
Plans for maintaining an agency's essential functions during any disruption, from a building fire to a pandemic. Every federal agency has one. See Chapter 6.
CONPLAN: Concept Plan
A military planning document that outlines how to respond to a specific scenario. These are routine planning tools, not secret activation orders.

D

Devolution (real)
The transfer of essential functions to alternate personnel and facilities during an emergency. A standard component of COOP planning. See True Devolution.
Devolution (conspiracy theory)
Jon Herold's claim that Trump secretly transferred presidential power to the military. Bears no resemblance to real devolution. See Chapter 4.
DSCA: Defense Support of Civil Authorities
The military framework for providing support to civilian agencies during domestic emergencies. Always temporary, always under civilian control. See DSCA page.

E

ECG: Enduring Constitutional Government
The overarching concept that all continuity planning must preserve the constitutional form of government. It is not a secret program; it is the stated purpose of every continuity directive.
EO: Executive Order
A directive issued by the President to manage operations of the federal government. EOs are public record, published in the Federal Register, and have the force of law, but they cannot override the Constitution or acts of Congress.

F

FCD: Federal Continuity Directive
Directives issued by DHS/FEMA that establish requirements for continuity planning across the federal government. FCD-1 is the framework; FCD-2 is the implementation guide. See FCD-1 page.
FEMA: Federal Emergency Management Agency
The agency within DHS that coordinates federal disaster response, recovery, and continuity planning. FEMA leads continuity training and exercises across all federal agencies.
FIOPs: Federal Interagency Operational Plans
Detailed operational plans that describe how the federal government delivers core capabilities during disasters. There are five, one for each mission area.
FMRS: Federal Mission Resilience Strategy
The strategy mandated by EO 13961 (2020) that every executive department had to implement into their continuity programs. This is what the EO actually did, not devolution.

I-J

ICS: Incident Command System
A standardized management system used for all emergency responses. Establishes a clear chain of command from the incident scene up through local, state, and federal levels.
Jus in bello
Latin for "law in war." The body of international law governing the conduct of armed conflict. This is what the DoD Law of War Manual covers, not domestic governance. See Chapter 5.

M

MOS: Military Occupational Specialty
A soldier's job code in the Army. Derek Johnson was a 14S (Air and Missile Defense Crewmember), a National Guard MOS that does not require a Top Secret clearance.
14S
Short Range Air Defense (SHORAD) / Air and Missile Defense Crewmember. A National Guard-specific MOS. Does not require Top Secret clearance. Has nothing to do with continuity of government.

N

NEF: National Essential Function
The most critical government functions that must be maintained at all times. Defined at the national level, these drive all subordinate continuity planning.
NIMS: National Incident Management System
A comprehensive, nationwide framework for managing incidents of all types. Provides common terminology, organizational structures, and processes.
NRF: National Response Framework
The guide for how the nation responds to all types of disasters and emergencies. Establishes roles and responsibilities across all levels of government. See Chapter 7.

P

PEADs: Presidential Emergency Action Documents
Pre-drafted presidential orders (executive orders, proclamations, messages) prepared in advance for crisis scenarios. They've existed since the Eisenhower administration. Every president has them. They are classified, which is why Jon Herold could claim they said whatever he wanted. See Chapter 4.
PMEF: Primary Mission Essential Function
Agency-specific functions that directly support the National Essential Functions. Each department identifies its own PMEFs based on its statutory mission.
Posse Comitatus Act (1878)
18 U.S.C. § 1385. Prohibits the use of federal military forces for domestic law enforcement, with limited exceptions. This is the law that makes Derek Johnson's "martial law" claims unconstitutional.
PPD: Presidential Policy Directive
A directive from the President establishing national policy on a specific topic. PPD-40 governs continuity planning. PPD-8 governs national preparedness.

Q-R

Q Clearance
A Department of Energy security clearance. Has nothing to do with military intelligence, the CIA, or the NSA. The fact that "Q" claimed this clearance reveals a misunderstanding of the classification system. See Chapter 9.
Reconstitution
The process of returning to normal operations after an emergency. Includes post-event analysis, lessons learned, and plan updates. It is a planned, orderly process, not a dramatic "reveal."

S-T

Stafford Act
The Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act. The primary federal law governing disaster response. Authorizes the President to declare disasters and provide federal assistance to state and local governments.
Title 10
Federal military service. When National Guard units are federalized under Title 10, they fall under the President's command and operate as part of the active military.
Title 32
State military service. National Guard units operating under Title 32 remain under the Governor's control and authority. This is the normal status for most Guard operations.
TS/SCI: Top Secret / Sensitive Compartmented Information
The highest levels of security clearance. Required to access continuity of government protocols and classified emergency plans. Derek Johnson's MOS (14S) did not require this clearance.

U

UCMJ: Uniform Code of Military Justice
The foundation of military law in the United States. Governs the conduct of all military members. Updated by the Military Justice Act of 2016.
