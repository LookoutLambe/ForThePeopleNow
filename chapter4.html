CHAPTER 4

Devolution Theory

Examining the claims with evidence and context

How It Started: A Newsweek Article

In March 2020, Newsweek published a dramatic headline: "Exclusive: Inside The Military's Top Secret Plans If Coronavirus Cripples the Government." It was actually a legitimate question — what happens if a pandemic takes out enough government officials that the federal government can't function? That's a real concern, and there are real plans for it (we cover them in Chapter 6).

Jon Herold, posting under the name "Patel Patriot," took that article and ran with it. By July 2021, he had launched his Devolution series on Substack. It went viral. Over 115,000 subscribers. No one was offering counterarguments because no one with actual expertise was paying attention yet.

And that's the problem. When a theory goes unchallenged, people mistake silence for validation.

The Bait and Switch

Here's what Jon did that most people missed: he changed the subject.

The Newsweek article was about pandemic continuity planning — what happens if COVID takes out government leadership. That's a legitimate topic with decades of established frameworks.

But Jon's series didn't stay there. It pivoted to the 2020 election. Suddenly it wasn't about pandemic continuity anymore — it was about Trump secretly using military authority to counter election fraud. The pandemic scenario was just the entry point. The real product was a narrative about a stolen election and a secret military operation.

Watch the shift: Jon starts with a real question (pandemic continuity) and real documents (executive orders, military directives). Then he reinterprets those documents to claim they prove something they don't — a secret military plan to keep Trump in power. By the time you notice the switch, you've already accepted the premise.

The PEADs Trick: How He Got Away With It

Here's what made Jon Herold stand out from every other conspiracy theorist: PEADs.

Presidential Emergency Action Documents (PEADs) are classified presidential directives prepared in advance for use during extraordinary emergencies. They are draft executive orders, proclamations, and messages designed to be ready for the President to sign during a crisis. Here's the critical detail: PEADs are classified. Their contents are not publicly available.

And that's exactly what Jon exploited.

Because PEADs are classified, no one could verify or debunk what Jon claimed they said. He could attribute whatever he wanted to these documents, and his audience had no way to check. It was the perfect scheme: cite a real category of classified documents, make up what they say, and no one can prove you wrong because the documents are secret.

Most people had never heard of PEADs before Jon's series. They didn't know these were simply pre-drafted emergency action documents — the government's equivalent of having a template ready in case of emergency. PEADs are not secret orders activating military operations. They are not hidden instructions for devolution. They are administrative preparation documents that exist so the President doesn't have to draft an executive order from scratch during a nuclear attack.

But because Jon was the first to bring PEADs into the patriot community conversation, and because nobody understood what they actually were, he got away with it. He became the "PEAD guy." He built his brand on documents no one could read. And he made millions doing it.

What Jon implied: PEADs contain secret instructions for military devolution that prove Trump is still in control. Because they're classified, only those "in the know" can understand what's really happening.
What PEADs actually are: Pre-drafted presidential emergency documents — templates for executive orders, proclamations, and messages prepared in advance for crisis scenarios. They've existed since the Eisenhower administration. Every president has them. They are administrative preparation, not secret military instructions. The Brennan Center for Justice has published extensive research on PEADs.
Jon Herold's genius — and it is genius in a con-artist sense — was finding classified documents that nobody in his audience had ever heard of, and then telling them what those documents "really" said. Since the documents are classified, his claims are unfalsifiable. You can't prove him wrong because you can't read them. But the Brennan Center, the Congressional Research Service, and actual national security professionals have all described what PEADs are, and they bear no resemblance to Jon's claims.

In His Own Words: Jon Herold's Claims vs. Reality

Let's use Jon's own words from his Devolution Part 1 (July 2, 2021) and put them next to the facts. He says "question everything" — so let's take him up on that.

Jon Herold writes: "The one conclusion of mine that hasn't changed and that I have only grown more confident in, is that Donald Trump initiated a continuity of government plan."
The Facts: Continuity of Government plans are not "initiated" by a president like a secret weapon. They are standing plans maintained by every administration since Eisenhower. FEMA manages them under PPD-40. They are civilian-led, constitutionally bound, and designed to preserve — not circumvent — the existing government. See Chapter 6 for the actual documents.
Jon Herold writes: "I'm just a guy trying to figure out what the heck is going on."
The Reality: He's "just a guy" with zero emergency management training, zero military experience, zero legal education, and zero government service — who charges money for a paid Substack subscription while telling 115,000+ followers he's cracked the code on classified government operations. "Just a guy" who blocks anyone with actual credentials who challenges him.
Jon Herold writes: "Biden has no meaningful operational control over the military... the team operating 'devolution' won't let any real harm come to us while the battlefield is prepped for whatever reveal is coming."
The Facts: Biden was recognized as Commander in Chief by the entire Joint Chiefs of Staff on January 12, 2021. Every military unit follows his orders. Every military operation since January 20, 2021 has been conducted under his authority. No military leader — active or retired — has ever produced evidence of a parallel command structure. The "reveal" has been "coming" for over 4 years. It never arrives.
Jon Herold writes: "Our military intelligence monitored and captured the election theft. We just need to expose the election fraud on a massive scale."
The Facts: 63 lawsuits were filed challenging the 2020 election. 61 were decided in favor of upholding the results. No court — including those with Trump-appointed judges — found evidence of fraud sufficient to overturn any state's results. The Joint Chiefs recognized Biden. The military participated in Biden's inauguration. If they "captured" the theft, why did they certify the results?
Jon Herold writes: "If PDJT had information that the Democrats would steal the election with help from China, that would be considered an act of war. Do you think that is something Trump would allow? Would our military let China install a puppet president?"
The Facts: This is a rhetorical question designed to lead you to a predetermined conclusion. It's not evidence. The ODNI (Office of the Director of National Intelligence) assessed foreign threats to the 2020 election and published their findings. China was identified as seeking to influence, not as having successfully stolen, the election. See Chapter 3 for the actual intelligence assessment.

What an Emergency Manager Actually Sees

As someone with a Master's in Emergency Management, here's what stands out when you read Jon's series with professional eyes:

  • He ignores the National Continuity Implementation Plan (NCIP) — the actual framework that governs how government continuity works. It's built on NIMS (National Incident Management System) and it's civilian-led. Not military.
  • He doesn't mention COOP (Continuity of Operations) — the programs that every federal agency maintains for exactly the scenarios he describes. These are well-documented, unclassified programs.
  • He treats routine executive orders as secret instructions — orders that simply update existing continuity frameworks that have existed since Eisenhower.
  • He proposes military takeover of civilian authority — which violates every principle of emergency management, the Posse Comitatus Act, and the Constitution itself.

The Continuity of Government Commission published two reports — "Preserving Our Institutions" (2003) and "The Continuity of the Presidency" — that address exactly the scenarios Jon describes. He either didn't read them, or he chose to ignore them because they contradict his narrative.

Here's the Irony: Devolution IS Real

Here's what makes this whole thing so frustrating. Devolution is a real thing. It's an actual, documented component of Continuity of Operations (COOP) planning. FEMA defines it. Federal Continuity Directives describe it. Emergency managers study it in graduate school.

Real devolution is the process of transferring essential functions to alternate personnel and facilities during an emergency. It's civilian-led. It's constitutional. It's boring. And it looks nothing like what Jon Herold describes.

Jon Herold's "Devolution": Trump secretly transferred military control of the government to maintain power after the 2020 election, with Biden serving as a figurehead while a shadow military operation runs the country.
Real Devolution (FEMA): A documented COOP procedure where essential functions are transferred to alternate civilian personnel at alternate facilities during emergencies, with full respect for constitutional processes. See True Devolution and FCD-1 (PDF).

Jon took a real term, stripped it of its actual meaning, and repackaged it as a conspiracy theory. That's not research — that's malinformation (real information used out of context to mislead). And the influencers who amplified it knew exactly what they were doing.

23 Articles and He Still Can't Define It

Here's something that should tell you everything you need to know: Jon Herold wrote 23 installments of his Devolution series and never once properly defined what devolution actually is.

In emergency management, devolution has a specific, documented definition. FEMA defines it. FCD-1 describes the process. There are checklists, templates, training courses, and exercises built around it. Any emergency management professional can define it in one sentence.

Jon can't. Because what he's describing isn't devolution — it's fanfiction. It's hypothetical sci-fi role-play dressed up with real document numbers to give it the appearance of legitimacy. Over 23 articles, he constructed an elaborate alternate reality — and thousands of well-meaning patriots got swindled by it.

They didn't get swindled because they're stupid. They got swindled because Jon mixed real document citations with fictional interpretations, and no one with actual expertise was there to say "that's not what that document means." Until now.

The Amplification Network: Revenue Over Truth

Jon Herold didn't build his following alone. He was amplified by a network of influencers who pushed the devolution narrative to massive audiences. And here's the part they don't want you to think about: the revenue mattered more than the truth.

  • We The Media — pushed devolution to their large Telegram and social media following, treating it as established fact rather than unverified theory. No fact-checking. No opposing views. Just amplification.
  • X22 Report — did perhaps the most damage. X22 repeatedly stated that devolution was real, telling listeners to "trust the plan" episode after episode. What X22 never told the audience: devolution is real — just not Jon's version. The real version is a mundane FEMA continuity process, not a secret military operation. X22 either knew this and didn't care, or never bothered to check. Either way, the audience paid the price.
  • Paid Substack subscriptions — Jon charges for premium content. The theory has been monetized from the start. When you profit from keeping people hooked on a narrative, there's zero incentive to admit you were wrong.

These platforms pushed the theory because it drove clicks, subscribers, and donations. When your business model depends on keeping people engaged with a narrative, accuracy becomes secondary to audience retention. Truth doesn't pay the bills — engagement does. And nothing drives engagement like telling people a secret military operation is about to be revealed.

The cruelest part of this ecosystem is that it keeps well-meaning patriots passive. If you believe the military is secretly handling everything, why vote? Why run for school board? Why attend city council meetings? The devolution narrative turns active citizens into passive consumers of content — content that someone else is profiting from.

The Echo Chamber Problem

One of the most dangerous things about the devolution community isn't the theory itself — it's how the community treats it. On TruthSocial and Telegram, people Re-Truth Jon's posts without verifying a single claim. Anyone who asks questions gets attacked. Anyone who presents counter-evidence gets called a shill or a fed.

That's not how truth works. Real research welcomes scrutiny. If your theory can't survive someone asking "but have you considered..." then it's not a theory — it's a belief system. And belief systems don't need evidence. That should concern you.
