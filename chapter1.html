The Constitution: Why It Matters

Everything on this site starts and ends with one document: the Constitution of the United States.

It's not just a piece of paper in a museum. It's the operating system of our country. It establishes three branches of government with checks and balances specifically designed to prevent any one person or group from grabbing too much power. The Bill of Rights — the first ten amendments — guarantees your freedoms: speech, religion, the press, assembly, the right to bear arms.

Here's what's important to understand: every theory we debunk on this site violates these principles. The devolution theory proposes transferring power to the military outside constitutional processes. The "law of war" claims suggest the Constitution has been suspended. The sovereign citizen theory claims the entire government is illegitimate. All of these ideas, if they were true, would mean the Constitution has failed — and that's exactly what foreign adversaries want Americans to believe.

We examined two prevalent theories — devolution and the law of war — against the actual evidence and constitutional frameworks. Our conclusion: these theories are not unfolding within our nation. The following chapters present the evidence, document by document, showing why.
