How It All Started: One Newsweek Article and a Lot of Imagination

In March 2020, Newsweek published a dramatic headline: "Exclusive: Inside The Military's Top Secret Plans If Coronavirus Cripples the Government." It was actually a legitimate question: what happens if a pandemic takes out enough government officials that the federal government can't function? That is a real concern, and there are real plans for it (we cover them in Chapter 6).

Most normal people read that headline and thought, "huh, interesting" and moved on with their lives. Jon Herold read it and apparently thought, "I bet I can build an entire media empire out of this." And he did. From a single Newsweek article, he spun up a 25-part series, a weekly podcast, a media company, and enough paid subscriptions to buy a house. All without ever taking a single FEMA course, reading a single Federal Continuity Directive in a professional setting, or speaking to anyone who actually works in continuity planning. Well, except for General Flynn, who told him it was nonsense. But who listens to three-star generals when there are Substack subscribers to collect?

Jon Herold, posting under the name "Patel Patriot," took that article and ran with it. By July 2021, he had launched his Devolution series on Substack. It went viral and attracted over 115,000 subscribers. No one was offering counterarguments because no one with actual expertise was paying attention yet.

The Bait and Switch

Here's what Jon did that most people missed: he changed the subject.

The Newsweek article was about pandemic continuity planning, specifically what happens if COVID takes out government leadership. That's a legitimate topic with decades of established frameworks.

But Jon's series didn't stay there. It pivoted to the 2020 election. Suddenly it wasn't about pandemic continuity anymore. It was about Trump secretly using military authority to counter election fraud. The pandemic scenario was just the entry point. The real product was a narrative about a stolen election and a secret military operation.

Watch the shift: Jon starts with a real question (pandemic continuity) and real documents (executive orders, military directives). Then he reinterprets those documents to claim they prove something they don't, namely a secret military plan to keep Trump in power. By the time you notice the switch, you've already accepted the premise.

The PEADs Trick: How He Got Away With It

Here's what made Jon Herold stand out from every other conspiracy theorist: PEADs.

Presidential Emergency Action Documents (PEADs) are pre-drafted executive orders, proclamations, and messages to Congress prepared for potential emergency scenarios. According to the Brennan Center for Justice, the practice originated during the Eisenhower administration as part of nuclear war contingency planning. As of 2017, there were 56 PEADs documented. They've been revised across administrations but remain classified. No PEAD has ever been declassified or leaked publicly, and there is no evidence they have ever been shared with relevant congressional committees.

And that's exactly what Jon exploited.

Because PEADs are classified, no one could verify or debunk what Jon claimed they said. He could attribute whatever he wanted to these documents, and his audience had no way to check. It was the perfect scheme: cite a real category of classified documents, make up what they say, and no one can prove you wrong because the documents are secret.

Most people had never heard of PEADs before Jon's series. They didn't know these were simply pre-drafted emergency action documents, the government's equivalent of having a template ready in case of emergency. PEADs are not secret orders activating military operations. They are not hidden instructions for devolution. They are administrative preparation documents that exist so the President doesn't have to draft an executive order from scratch during a nuclear attack.

But because Jon was the first to bring PEADs into the patriot community conversation, and because nobody understood what they actually were, he got away with it. He became the "PEAD guy." He built his brand on documents no one could read. And he made millions doing it.

Think about that for a moment. A man with no security clearance, no government experience, and no way to access classified documents built an entire career telling people what those classified documents say. It is the equivalent of someone who has never been inside Fort Knox telling you exactly how much gold is in there and charging you $5 a month for the "intel." You cannot verify it. He cannot verify it. Nobody can verify it. That is the point. That is the business model.

Jon claims: Presidential Emergency Action Documents (PEADs) are proof of devolution. They contain secret instructions for military takeover. Because they are classified, nobody can disprove what he says they contain. He built his entire brand on this single claim.

What PEADs actually are: Pre-drafted executive orders, proclamations, and messages to Congress prepared for emergency scenarios. The practice started under Eisenhower for nuclear war contingency planning. As of 2017, there were 56 documented PEADs. They have been revised across administrations but remain classified. No PEAD has ever been declassified or leaked. There is zero evidence they have ever been shared with relevant congressional committees, let alone Substack bloggers. The Brennan Center for Justice and the Congressional Research Service both address PEADs in the context of emergency preparedness. Neither describes a mechanism for secretly transferring power. PEADs are emergency templates. They are the government equivalent of having a form letter ready in case of crisis. Jon turned form letters into a media empire.

What Jon implied: PEADs contain secret instructions for military devolution that prove Trump is still in control. Because they're classified, only those "in the know" can understand what's really happening.
What PEADs actually are: Pre-drafted presidential emergency documents. These are templates for executive orders, proclamations, and messages prepared in advance for crisis scenarios. They've existed since the Eisenhower administration. Every president has them. They are administrative preparation, not secret military instructions. The Brennan Center for Justice has published extensive research on PEADs.
Jon Herold's genius, and it is genius in a con-artist sense, was finding classified documents that nobody in his audience had ever heard of, and then telling them what those documents "really" said. Since the documents are classified, his claims are unfalsifiable. You can't prove him wrong because you can't read them. But the Brennan Center, the Congressional Research Service, and actual national security professionals have all described what PEADs are, and they bear no resemblance to Jon's claims.

His Claims vs. The Actual Documents

This is where the theory falls apart completely. Let's put Jon's claims next to the actual government documents he misinterprets. He says "question everything," so let's take him up on that.

Jon's Claim: Devolution Part 1 (July 2, 2021)

Jon Herold writes: "The one conclusion of mine that hasn't changed and that I have only grown more confident in, is that Donald Trump initiated a continuity of government plan."

Reality: FEMA Guide to Continuity of Government

The actual FEMA Guide to Continuity of Government is a publicly available document that outlines how COG planning works. It describes an orderly, documented, multi-agency process with specific protocols, training requirements, and oversight. Continuity of Government plans are not "initiated" by a president like a secret weapon. They are standing plans maintained by every administration since Eisenhower. FEMA manages them under PPD-40. They are civilian-led, constitutionally bound, and designed to preserve, not circumvent, the existing government. See Chapter 6 for the actual documents.

Jon Herold writes: "Biden has no meaningful operational control over the military... the team operating 'devolution' won't let any real harm come to us while the battlefield is prepped for whatever reveal is coming."
Reality: CONPLAN - Continuity of Operations Plan

Every single military operation conducted since January 20, 2021 was authorized by Biden as Commander in Chief. Every deployment. Every strike. Every troop movement. The entire Joint Chiefs recognized him on January 12, 2021. Not one general, not one admiral, not one senior NCO has ever produced evidence of a parallel command structure. If the military were secretly operating under Trump, every officer who followed Biden's orders committed treason under the UCMJ. Two million service members. Zero whistleblowers. Zero leaked memos. Zero defections. The "reveal" Jon promised has been "coming" for over four years. Ask yourself honestly: how many more years will you wait?

Jon Herold writes: "Our military intelligence monitored and captured the election theft. We just need to expose the election fraud on a massive scale."
Reality: National Security Act of 1947

The National Security Act of 1947 defines the structure and authority of U.S. military intelligence. It does not authorize domestic election surveillance. 63 lawsuits were filed challenging the 2020 election. 61 were decided in favor of upholding the results. No court, including those with Trump-appointed judges, found evidence of fraud sufficient to overturn any state's results. The Joint Chiefs recognized Biden. The military participated in Biden's inauguration. If they "captured" the theft, why did they certify the results?

Jon Herold writes (Part 4): "Sometime between December 7th (When Trump issued executive order 13961) and January 6th, 2021, President Donald Trump used a Presidential Emergency Action Document (PEAD) to suspend the counting of the electoral college votes."
Reality: Presidential Succession Act

The electoral votes were counted. On live television. Congress certified them. Biden was inaugurated. The military participated in the inauguration ceremony. The Secret Service transferred protection. The nuclear codes were handed over. Every single step of the constitutional transfer of power happened exactly as the Constitution prescribes. Nothing was suspended. If Trump used a PEAD to stop the electoral count, it did not work, which means either the PEAD failed (impossible if the military is secretly in control) or it never happened. Jon picks option three: it happened but you cannot see it yet. That is not analysis. That is a religion.

Jon's Claim: EO 13961 is the "Devo EO"

Jon calls EO 13961 the "Devo EO" and claims it created a "shadow government" through the Federal Mission Resilience Executive Committee. He emphasizes that the EO cites the National Security Act of 1947 (Title 50, War and National Defense) rather than disaster preparedness, claiming this proves it's a war plan.

Reality: The Continuity Guidance Circular

EO 13961 cites the National Security Act of 1947 because continuity of government IS a national security function. It always has been, since the Eisenhower era. The NSA 1947 (PDF) created the National Security Council, the CIA, and unified the military departments. It's an organizational law, not a war authorization. Every continuity directive since Eisenhower cites it because that's the legal framework national security operates under. EO 13961 was an enhancement of emergency management that integrated continuity planning with enterprise risk management. It enabled telework as a continuity function and ultimately led to FCD-0 in 2024 that strengthened the continuity framework. That's not a shadow government. It's better emergency management. And on January 19, 2025, Jon himself admitted it was over.

Jon Herold writes: EO 13848 gives the Secretary of Defense authority to report directly to the President on foreign interference and allows "remedial actions" beyond sanctions, implying military intervention in elections. He claims voting machines use 20% Chinese components.
Reality: The Actual EO 13848

Read the executive order yourself. It is right here (PDF). It literally states "there has been no evidence of a foreign power altering the outcome or vote tabulation." The document Jon cites as proof of election interference explicitly says there was no election interference. The "remedial actions" he gets excited about are sanctions, export restrictions, and asset freezing, the same tools used against Russia and Iran for decades. Not military operations. Not martial law. Sanctions. And Dominion Voting Systems won a $787 million defamation settlement against Fox News for spreading the exact same claims about voting machines that Jon repeats. Fox paid three-quarters of a billion dollars because the claims were false.

Jon's Claim: Trump officials who left government and founded organizations like America First Policy Institute are "distributed nodes of governmental authority" -- a shadow government operating through the Federal Mission Resilience Strategy.

Former government officials starting think tanks is what every administration's people do when they leave office. Obama officials started the Obama Foundation. Bush officials started consulting firms. Clinton officials went to Wall Street. Trump officials started the America First Policy Institute. This is not a shadow government. This is Washington, D.C. People leave government and start organizations. It has happened after every single administration in modern history. Jon saw this completely normal pattern and concluded it was a clandestine military operation. By his logic, every former official who ever started a nonprofit is part of a shadow government. That is not analysis. That is seeing patterns in clouds and charging people $5 a month to hear about them.

The Facts: Jon literally admits no one knows what's in PEADs, including himself, then builds his entire theory on speculation about their contents. The word "approximately" in a budget document means the exact number was being reviewed, not that Trump added secret war plans. PEADs are routinely reviewed and updated across administrations. Here's the Irony: Devolution IS Real

Here's what makes this whole thing so frustrating. Devolution is a real thing. It's an actual, documented component of Continuity of Operations (COOP) planning. FEMA defines it. Federal Continuity Directives describe it. Emergency managers study it in graduate school.

Real devolution is the process of transferring essential functions to alternate personnel and facilities during an emergency. It's civilian-led. It's constitutional. It's boring. And it looks nothing like what Jon Herold describes.

Jon Herold's "Devolution": Trump secretly transferred military control of the government to maintain power after the 2020 election, with Biden serving as a figurehead while a shadow military operation runs the country.
Real Devolution (FEMA): A documented COOP procedure where essential functions are transferred to alternate civilian personnel at alternate facilities during emergencies, with full respect for constitutional processes. See True Devolution and FCD-1 (PDF).

Jon took a real term, stripped it of its actual meaning, and repackaged it as a conspiracy theory. That's not research. That's malinformation (real information used out of context to mislead). And the influencers who amplified it knew exactly what they were doing.

Jon Herold wrote 23 installments of his Devolution series and never once properly defined what devolution actually is. In emergency management, devolution has a specific, documented definition. FEMA defines it. FCD-1 describes the process. There are checklists, templates, training courses, and exercises built around it. Any emergency management professional can define it in one sentence. Jon can't. Because what he's describing isn't devolution. It's fanfiction. It's hypothetical sci-fi role-play dressed up with real document numbers to give it the appearance of legitimacy.

What an Emergency Manager Actually Sees

As someone with a Master's in Emergency Management, here's what stands out when you read Jon's series with professional eyes:

  • He ignores the National Continuity Implementation Plan (NCIP), the actual framework that governs how government continuity works. It's built on NIMS (National Incident Management System) and it's civilian-led. Not military.
  • He doesn't mention COOP (Continuity of Operations), the programs that every federal agency maintains for exactly the scenarios he describes. These are well-documented, unclassified programs.
  • He treats routine executive orders as secret instructions, orders that simply update existing continuity frameworks that have existed since Eisenhower.
  • He proposes military takeover of civilian authority, which violates every principle of emergency management, the Posse Comitatus Act, and the Constitution itself.

The Continuity of Government Commission published two reports, "Preserving Our Institutions" (2003) and "The Continuity of the Presidency," that address exactly the scenarios Jon describes. He either didn't read them, or he chose to ignore them because they contradict his narrative.
