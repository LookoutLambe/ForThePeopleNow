The Constitution: The Starting Point for Everything

Everything on this site starts and ends with one document: the Constitution of the United States.

It's not just a piece of paper in a museum. It's the operating system of our country. It establishes three branches of government with checks and balances specifically designed to prevent any one person or group from seizing too much power.

Article I: Legislative

Congress makes the laws. The House and Senate provide representation. No single person can legislate alone. This is the first check on power.

Article II: Executive

The President executes the laws. Commander in Chief of the military — but bound by Congressional authority for war, funding, and oversight.

Article III: Judicial

The courts interpret the laws. The Supreme Court is the final arbiter. No theory, no executive order, no military regulation overrides a court ruling.

Bill of Rights

The first ten amendments guarantee your freedoms: speech, religion, press, assembly, bearing arms, due process. These cannot be suspended by executive order.
