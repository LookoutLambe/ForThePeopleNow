CHAPTER 4 — THE BOOK

The Danger of the Devolution Theory

By Chris Lambe — A pro-Trump, anti-devolution examination
Before you read further: This is not an anti-Trump position. This is a pro-Constitution, pro-truth position written by a veteran who supports Trump. The devolution theory actually hurts the very cause it claims to support. Here's why.

Why This Matters to You

If you've spent any time on TruthSocial, Telegram, or patriot channels, you've probably heard about "devolution." Maybe someone shared a post saying Trump secretly transferred power to the military. Maybe you watched a video claiming executive orders prove we're under military control. Maybe it sounded convincing because the person throwing around document numbers seemed like they knew what they were talking about.

Here's the problem: they don't.

The devolution theory, promoted by Jon Herold (a.k.a. "Patel Patriot") on his Substack with over 115,000 subscribers, is a tangled web of misread documents, out-of-context quotes, and fundamental misunderstandings of how government actually works. And I'm not saying this as some liberal critic — I'm saying this as a veteran with a Master's degree in Emergency Management from Trident University who has actually studied the very documents Jon claims to interpret. I've taken the FEMA courses. I've read the Federal Continuity Directives. I know how COOP and COG actually work — because it's what I went to school for.

So What Does Devolution Actually Claim?

Let's break it down in plain English. The devolution theory makes five core claims:

  1. The "Deep State" Cabal: A powerful group of elites has been secretly controlling governments and undermining the Constitution for decades.
  2. Secret Military Operation: Trump and military intelligence launched a classified operation to remove this cabal from power.
  3. Power Transfer to Military: Authority over civilian national security was "devolved" (transferred) to military control to prevent sabotage by the Deep State.
  4. "White Hat" Operatives: People like Gen. Michael Flynn are secretly working with Trump to execute this plan.
  5. "The Storm": At some point, there will be mass arrests, military tribunals, and shocking revelations.

Sound dramatic? That's by design. But here's what the evidence actually shows...

The "Evidence" Falls Apart

From the outset, Jon Herold has never produced any direct, verifiable evidence of devolution's core claims. Instead, the "proof" relies on:

  • Speculative analysis of vague statements and hand gestures
  • Minor staffing changes framed as signs of military takeover
  • Strained interpretations of routine policy updates
  • Reinterpreting old information in new ways whenever predictions fail

This is called confirmation bias — where you start with a conclusion and then twist everything to fit it. When you're looking for hidden messages, you'll find them everywhere, even where they don't exist.

"Once somebody has accepted a conspiracy theory, no amount of evidence is going to change their mind. The conspiracy theory has become a way of looking at the world." — Michael Barkun, leading expert on conspiracy theories

What Continuity of Government Actually Is

Here's where Jon really gets it wrong — and where emergency management expertise matters.

Continuity of Government (COG) is simply the government's plan to keep running if something catastrophic happens. Think nuclear attack, massive natural disaster, or pandemic. It's been around since the Eisenhower administration in the 1950s when everyone was worried about Soviet nukes.

COG involves:

  • Who takes over if the President is killed or incapacitated (line of succession)
  • How to keep essential services running during a crisis
  • Where officials would relocate if Washington D.C. was destroyed
  • How to restore normal operations after the crisis passes
Here's the critical part that Jon either doesn't understand or deliberately ignores: COG is designed to preserve the constitutional order, not overthrow it. It does not grant any extra-constitutional authority to the president, military, or anyone else to seize control outside of established law.

Every single COG document — and we have them in our Chapter 6: Continuity section — explicitly states that continuity operations must maintain "proper respect for the constitutional separation of powers" and the "rule of law."

Jon claims executive orders like EO 13961 prove devolution. But if you actually read the order (and we have it linked in Chapter 5), it simply updates existing continuity policies that have been in place across multiple administrations. There's no secret military takeover language. None. Zero.

Executive Orders: Tools, Not Conspiracy

Jon and his followers love throwing around executive order numbers like they're secret codes. Let's decode them with actual facts:

EO 13848 (2018)

Jon says this proves devolution. What it actually does: updates policies for responding to foreign interference in elections. The text literally states it "is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit" beyond existing laws. Read it yourself — it's in our document library.

EO 13961 (2020)

Jon says this enabled Trump to transfer power to the military. What it actually does: updates continuity of government policies to account for COVID-19 risks. These same types of updates have happened under every modern president. It's as routine as changing the batteries in your smoke detector.

EO 13912 (2020)

Jon says National Guard activations prove military control. What actually happened: the Guard was activated to help with pandemic response, which happens during every major emergency. Read the actual order.

The Military's Answer

If devolution were real, the military would have to be in on it. So what did the military actually say and do?

On January 12, 2021, the entire Joint Chiefs of Staff issued an unprecedented memo stating:

"On January 20th, 2021, in accordance with the Constitution, confirmed by the states and the courts, and certified by Congress, President-elect Biden will be inaugurated and will become our 46th Commander in Chief."

That's not ambiguous. That's not coded. The top military leaders in the country publicly stated they recognized Biden as the incoming commander-in-chief and pledged to uphold the Constitution.

No military official — active or retired — has ever produced documentation of a devolution plan. If thousands of military and intelligence personnel were involved in such a massive operation, it would be "virtually impossible for there to be no documented evidence from insiders or whistleblowers." History proves this — the Pentagon Papers leaked, Snowden's NSA revelations leaked, Abu Ghraib leaked. But devolution? Not a single shred of insider evidence in over four years.

What About Michael Flynn?

Jon claims Flynn is a secret "white hat" operative working behind the scenes. But look at what Flynn has actually been doing since leaving the administration:

  • Appearing regularly on conservative TV networks like Newsmax
  • Campaigning for Republican candidates
  • Giving paid public speeches
  • Joining advisory boards of activist organizations

These are the activities of a political commentator, not a covert operative awaiting secret orders. If Flynn were actually involved in a top-secret military operation, he wouldn't be doing interviews on cable TV.

Where's "The Storm"?

Devolution has been predicting imminent mass arrests and military tribunals since 2021. It's now 2026. Every single prediction has failed. Every deadline has passed. Every "this is it!" moment has fizzled.

The theory's response? Move the goalposts. Reinterpret the timeline. Find new "evidence." This is the hallmark of every failed prediction in every conspiracy theory throughout history.

The Real Harm

Here's why this isn't just harmless speculation:

  • It makes Trump supporters look uninformed. When people repeat debunked claims about military occupation and secret plans, it undermines the credibility of legitimate conservative positions.
  • It breeds passivity. If you believe the military is secretly handling everything, why get involved in local politics, school boards, or elections? The theory encourages people to sit back and "trust the plan" instead of doing the actual work of self-governance.
  • It erodes trust in real institutions. The Constitution, the courts, the military — these aren't perfect, but they're the framework that protects your rights. Conspiracy theories that claim they're all corrupted or secretly controlled weaken the very institutions patriots should be defending.
  • It makes money for grifters. Jon Herold charges for premium Substack content. This theory has been monetized. When someone profits from keeping you engaged with a narrative, question their motives.

What Should You Do Instead?

If you care about the Constitution and the future of this country (and we know you do — that's why you're here), here's what actually matters:

  • Read the actual documents. We've provided them throughout this site. Don't take anyone's word for what an executive order says — read it yourself.
  • Understand how government actually works. Our Chapter 6 on Continuity and Chapter 7 on Emergency Management explain the real frameworks.
  • Get involved locally. Run for school board. Attend city council meetings. Volunteer as a poll watcher. That's how you actually defend the republic.
  • Think critically. When someone makes a claim, ask: What's the actual evidence? Have I read the original source? Is this person making money from this narrative?
The Constitution doesn't need a secret military operation to save it. It needs informed citizens who understand it, defend it, and participate in the system of self-governance it established. That's what "For The People, By The People" actually means.
