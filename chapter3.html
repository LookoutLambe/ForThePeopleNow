CHAPTER 3

Foreign Information Warfare

How adversary nations weaponize information against American democracy

The War You Don't See

Here's something most Americans don't realize: there's a war being waged against you right now, and you're probably not even aware you're on the battlefield.

It's not fought with bullets or bombs. It's fought with memes, Telegram posts, YouTube videos, and social media accounts that look just like yours. Foreign governments, including Russia, China, and Iran, have figured out that they don't need to invade America to weaken it. They just need Americans to stop trusting each other and their own institutions.

And here's the uncomfortable truth: many of the conspiracy theories circulating in patriot communities are doing exactly what foreign adversaries want. Theories that tell you the government is illegitimate, the Constitution has been suspended, and you can't trust anything, that's not patriotism. That's the intended result of a foreign influence campaign.

Words Matter: Know the Difference

Misinformation: Wrong information shared by people who don't know it's wrong. Your uncle sharing a false meme because he thought it was real.

Disinformation: Wrong information spread deliberately to manipulate you. A Russian troll farm creating fake American accounts to spread division.

Malinformation: Real information taken out of context to mislead. Taking a real executive order and claiming it means something it doesn't. This is what Patel Patriot and Derek Johnson do.

Foreign Threat Actors

  • 🇷🇺 Russia: Internet Research Agency, troll farms, fake accounts on both sides
  • 🇨🇳 China: Global propaganda, media censorship, economic leverage
  • 🇮🇷 Iran: Cyber operations, voter data theft, threatening emails

Their Goal

  • Americans distrust their own government
  • Americans distrust each other
  • Americans stop participating in democracy

That third one, malinformation, is the most dangerous because it has a kernel of truth. When someone shows you a real document number but then tells you it means something completely different from what it actually says, it's harder to spot the lie.

Who's Doing This and Why

Russia

Russia wrote the playbook on this. The Internet Research Agency, a Kremlin-linked troll farm in St. Petersburg, ran a massive campaign during the 2016 election. They created fake American accounts on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram. They posed as both liberals and conservatives. Their goal wasn't to support one side; it was to get Americans fighting with each other.

They amplified the most extreme voices on both sides. They organized real protests and counter-protests in the same city on the same day. And they're still at it. Russia's goal is simple: undermine Americans' faith in democracy itself.

China

China plays the long game. They run global propaganda operations to promote their authoritarian model as superior to Western democracy. Within China, the internet is completely censored. Now they're expanding that influence abroad, pressuring foreign media companies to self-censor in exchange for access to the Chinese market. Their information warfare capabilities are growing fast.

Iran

Iran combines cyber attacks with information operations. In 2020, they were caught accessing U.S. voter registration data and sending threatening emails to voters. They target both sides of the political spectrum, looking for any wedge issue they can exploit.

The uncomfortable truth: Every conspiracy theory on this site, including devolution, law of war, sovereign citizen, and Q, accomplishes exactly what Russia, China, and Iran have spent billions trying to achieve: Americans distrusting their own government, their own courts, their own military chain of command, and their own elections. The Connection to Devolution and Law of War Theories

Now here's where this connects to everything else on this site. Ask yourself:

  • Who benefits when Americans believe their government is illegitimate?
  • Who benefits when Americans distrust the Constitution?
  • Who benefits when Americans believe the military has secretly taken over?
  • Who benefits when Americans sit on the sidelines waiting for a "secret plan" instead of participating in their democracy?

Not America. Russia, China, and Iran benefit. Every single one of these outcomes is exactly what foreign adversaries have been working toward for decades.

We're not saying Jon Herold or Derek Johnson are foreign agents. What we're saying is that the narratives they promote, that the government is fake, the Constitution is suspended, and you should just "trust the plan," produce exactly the same result that a Russian influence operation would.

Whether conspiracy theories originate from foreign troll farms or homegrown grifters, the effect is the same: Americans stop trusting their institutions, stop participating in governance, and become easier to manipulate. That's not patriotism. It's exactly what our adversaries want.

Your Role in This Fight

You're on the front lines whether you like it or not. Every time you share a post, you're either fighting disinformation or spreading it. Here's how to protect yourself:

  • Check the source. If someone cites a document, find and read the actual document. Don't take their word for what it says.
  • Ask who benefits. If a narrative makes you distrust your own government, military, and Constitution, who benefits from that?
  • Be suspicious of monetization. If someone charges you money to access "the truth" about secret government plans, they have a financial incentive to keep you hooked.
  • Look for opposing views. Credible researchers welcome scrutiny. Grifters attack anyone who questions them.
