CHAPTER 5 — SUB-PAGE

United States Space Force

The newest branch of the U.S. Armed Forces and its mission to secure American interests in space

Establishment

The United States Space Force was established on December 20, 2019, making it the first new military branch since the U.S. Air Force was created in 1947. Its creation represented a formal recognition that space had become a critical domain for national security.

Historical Background

Military involvement in space dates back to 1945, when the U.S. Army Air Forces commissioned the "Toward New Horizons" report, which anticipated the military significance of space. The Cold War space race accelerated military space capabilities and led to the establishment of several key organizations:

  • Air Force Space Command (1982) — consolidated Air Force space operations
  • Naval Space Command (1985) — managed Navy space activities
  • U.S. Space Command (1988) — unified combatant command for military space operations

Path to Creation

In February 2019, President Trump directed the Pentagon to begin reorganizing space operations into a new, independent military branch. The initiative received bipartisan Congressional support and was ultimately authorized through the National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA).

Mission

On September 6, 2023, the Space Force announced its formal mission statement: to "Secure our Nation's interests in, from, and to space." This mission encompasses three distinct domains:

  • In space: Achieving and maintaining space superiority
  • From space: Providing critical communications, navigation, and other space-based capabilities
  • To space: Maintaining launch infrastructure and access to space

Organizational Structure

The Space Force operates through a four-tier organizational structure:

  1. Headquarters — overall command and strategic direction
  2. Field Commands — major operational and support organizations
  3. Space Deltas — operational units focused on specific mission areas
  4. Squadrons — tactical-level units

Field Commands

The Space Force has three Field Commands:

  • Space Operations Command (SpOC) — responsible for generating, presenting, and sustaining space forces
  • Space Systems Command (SSC) — manages acquisition, development, and sustainment of space systems with a budget of approximately $15.6 billion
  • Space Training and Readiness Command (STARCOM) — activated on August 23, 2021, responsible for developing trained and ready space forces

Personnel and Identity

The Space Force is the smallest branch of the U.S. Armed Forces, with approximately 14,000 personnel. Its members are called "Guardians" and serve under the motto "Semper Supra" — Latin for "Always Above."

The Space Force represents the formal recognition of space as a warfighting domain. Its creation through bipartisan Congressional action and the NDAA demonstrates the normal, constitutional process by which the U.S. military adapts to new strategic realities.
